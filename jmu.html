How does JMU play a role in our community?

Community Service

As over 20,000 JMU students reside in Harrisonburg, James Madison University is a large contributor to the overall Harrisonburg community. JMU defines their civic engagement as,

"fostering mutually beneficial and reciprocal partnerships, ranging from local to global, that connect learning to practice, address critical societal problems and, improve quality of life."

Some opportunities for community service include "Adopt a Street," "JMU Community Service Learning," and "Trail Building and Maintenance with Shenandoah Valley Bicycle Coalition." There are countless ways to get involved within our large community.

Examples of some community service projects sponsored by JMU:

  • Rockingham-Harrisonburg SPCA
  • Virginia Quilt Museum
  • Big Brothers Big Sisters
  • R.I.S.E
  • Rockingham Memorial Hospital
  • Relay for Life
  • Lego League Tournament
  • Massanutten Regional Library
  • University Program Board and more!
